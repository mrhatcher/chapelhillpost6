Preamble to the Constitution of the American Legion

"For God and Country we associate ourselves together for the following purposes: To uphold and defend the Constitution of the United States of America; to maintain law and order; to foster and perpetuate a one-hundred percent Americanism; to preserve the memories and incidents of our association in the Great Wars; to inculcate a sense of individual obligation to the community, state and nation; to combat the autocracy of both the classes and the masses; to make right the master of might; to promote peace and good will on earth; to safeguard and transmit to posterity the principles of justice, freedom and democracy; to consecrate and sanctify our comradeship by our devotion to mutual helpfulness."

🇺🇸

🎖️

Our Mission

Our mission is to further promote the interests of our organization and our members by fulfilling the mission of the American Legion, as stated in our constitution's preamble, within our community.

Chapel Hill Post No. 6 has been living this mission since 1919 — serving veterans, supporting families, and strengthening the community we call home.

👥

Growing Our Membership

We seek to add new members so that we will be able to grow and serve area veterans — both members and non-members.

A larger, stronger Post means more resources to serve veterans in Chapel Hill and Orange County. Whether you're a veteran yourself or a family member of one who served, there's a place for you at Post No. 6.

01

Veterans Affairs & Rehabilitation

Ensuring every veteran receives the care and benefits they have earned through their service. The American Legion has been the leading advocate for veterans' rights since its founding in 1919.

Post No. 6 provides direct assistance through our accredited Veterans Service Officer (VSO), who helps members and non-members navigate VA claims, disability compensation, pension benefits, and healthcare enrollment — all at no charge.

02

National Security

Supporting a strong, well-prepared national defense. The American Legion believes that a robust military capability is essential to preserving peace and protecting the freedoms that veterans fought to defend.

Post No. 6 advocates for adequate defense funding, proper care for active-duty service members, and policies that honor the sacrifices of those who wear the uniform today and those who have worn it in the past.

03

Americanism

Promoting patriotism, youth programs, and civic responsibility throughout the community. Americanism means fostering love of country, respect for the flag, and active participation in the democratic process.

Post No. 6 promotes Americanism through our Boys State scholarship program, the Oratorical Contest, flag retirement ceremonies, the Speakers' Bureau, and community events that celebrate American values and history.

04

Children & Youth

Investing in the next generation through scouting, Boys State, and scholarships. The American Legion believes that strong youth programs are the foundation of a strong nation.

Post No. 6 sponsors Boy Scout Troops 505 and Crew 505, funds Boys and Girls State leadership scholarships, hosts the annual Oratorical Contest, and supports youth baseball — giving Chapel Hill's young people the skills, values, and opportunities they need to become tomorrow's leaders.

Veteran Resources

Help & Resources

Post No. 6 connects veterans and their families with the benefits, support, and services they've earned. Select a topic below to learn more.

🪦

Headstones & Grave Markers

The Department of Veterans Affairs provides headstones, grave markers, and medallions to memorialize eligible veterans. This benefit is available at no cost to the veteran's family.

How to apply: Submit VA Form 40-1330 (Application for Standard Government Headstone or Marker). Post No. 6's Veterans Service Officer can assist with completing the form. Contact us at adjutant@alpost6.org for help.

🍊

Agent Orange Claims

Veterans who were exposed to Agent Orange during their military service may be entitled to disability compensation, health care, and other benefits from the VA.

Navy veterans: The VA maintains a database of ships that operated in Vietnam-era waters. If your ship is on the list, you may qualify for Agent Orange-related benefits even if you never set foot on Vietnamese soil. Our VSO can help you check your eligibility and file a claim.

🎖️

VA Benefits & Veterans Service Officer

Post No. 6 has an accredited Veterans Service Officer (VSO) who can assist veterans and their families at no charge with VA claims, appeals, pension applications, and other benefits.

Services include: disability compensation claims, survivor benefits (DIC), GI Bill education benefits, VA home loan certificates, and healthcare enrollment. Contact the Post to schedule an appointment with our VSO.

Schedule VSO Appointment
💰

Financial Aid for Veterans

Veterans and their families facing financial hardship may be eligible for assistance through the American Legion, the VA, and other programs. Post No. 6 can connect you with the right resources.

Available programs include: Temporary Financial Assistance (TFA) for families with children, the American Legion's National Emergency Fund, state and local emergency assistance programs, and VA pension benefits for low-income veterans.

Request Assistance Info
👨‍👩‍👧

Military Families & Support Groups

UNC-Chapel Hill offers group therapy and counseling services specifically for military families, veterans, and their loved ones. These services are available through the UNC Department of Psychiatry.

Post No. 6 also connects military families with community resources, peer support networks, and referrals to local mental health professionals experienced in veteran and military family issues. Contact the Post for current program schedules and enrollment information.

🇺🇸

Flag Etiquette

The American Legion has long been the guardian of proper U.S. flag etiquette. Key guidelines include: the flag should never touch the ground; it should be displayed from sunrise to sunset; and it should be illuminated if displayed at night.

Post No. 6 conducts respectful flag retirement ceremonies for worn or damaged flags. Bring your worn flag to any Post meeting and we will ensure it is retired with the dignity it deserves. Contact us for our next scheduled flag retirement ceremony date.

⚰️

Funeral & Memorial Assistance

Post No. 6's Honor Guard is available to perform military funeral honors for veterans in the Chapel Hill area. The Honor Guard provides a dignified ceremony including rifle salute and flag folding and presentation.

The VA also provides burial benefits including burial in a national cemetery, burial allowance, and a Presidential Memorial Certificate. Our VSO can assist families in applying for these benefits. To request Honor Guard services, contact the Post as early as possible.

Request Honor Guard Services
📋

Membership Information

Any honorably discharged veteran who served on active federal service since December 7, 1941 is eligible to join the American Legion. All branches welcome — Army, Navy, Marines, Air Force, Coast Guard, and Space Force.

Annual dues at Post No. 6 are $60/year for Legion members. You can join online through MyLegion.org or contact the Post for a paper application. Family members may be eligible for the Auxiliary or SAL.

Preserving Military History

Post 6 Museum

A volunteer-supported museum preserving and displaying military history for the Chapel Hill community and beyond.

🏛️

About Post 6 Museum

The Post 6 Museum is a volunteer-supported institution dedicated to preserving and displaying the military history of veterans from Chapel Hill and the surrounding area. It serves as a living memorial to those who have served.

Located at the Post No. 6 facility at 3700 NC Hwy 54 West, the museum welcomes public visitors and accepts donations of military items, memorabilia, and artifacts that help tell the story of American service members across generations.

🕐

Visiting the Museum

The Post 6 Museum is open to the public. The museum is housed within the Post No. 6 facility and is best visited during Post events and monthly meetings when the building is fully staffed.

Location: 3700 NC Highway 54 West, Chapel Hill, NC 27516
Best times to visit: Monthly Post meetings (2nd Tuesday, starting 5:30 PM) and scheduled Post events.
Contact the museum director to arrange a private visit or group tour.

Wartime Memorabilia

The museum's memorabilia collection spans multiple conflicts and eras — from World War II through modern campaigns. Items include uniforms, medals, insignia, personal effects, and equipment from veterans who served in every branch of the armed forces.

Each item in the collection carries with it a personal story of service and sacrifice. The museum works to document and preserve the history behind every artifact, ensuring future generations understand the human cost of freedom.

📚

Books & Historical Documents

The museum maintains a collection of military history books, unit histories, official documents, photographs, letters, and diaries that provide invaluable primary and secondary source material about military history.

These materials are available for viewing at the museum and support research by historians, students, and family members tracing their veteran ancestors. Contact the museum director to arrange access to specific materials.

✈️

Military Models & Artwork

The museum displays a collection of scale military models — aircraft, ships, vehicles, and equipment — alongside original military artwork and photography that illustrates the history of American military service.

These displays bring military history to life for visitors of all ages, making the museum an excellent educational resource for school groups, scouts, and families interested in America's military heritage.

🤝

How to Donate Items

The Post 6 Museum welcomes donations of military memorabilia, books, models, artwork, photographs, documents, and other historically significant items related to military service.

Before donating, please contact Museum Director Greg Smith or Ed Gill through the Post adjutant to discuss the item and ensure it fits the museum's collection needs. All donations are acknowledged and donors are recognized for their contributions to preserving history.

Who We Are

Organizations

🌟

American Legion Auxiliary

A separate women's organization operating alongside Post No. 6. Open to spouses, daughters, granddaughters, sisters, mothers, and grandmothers of eligible veterans.

The Auxiliary is one of the largest patriotic women's organizations in the world, dedicated to supporting veterans, military, and their families. The Unit donates to both veterans in need and to the Post itself, helping fund programs and community outreach.

⭐

Sons of the American Legion (SAL)

The SAL is open to male descendants of veterans — sons, grandsons, great-grandsons, stepsons, and adopted sons of eligible American Legion members or deceased veterans.

The SAL continues the Legion's legacy across generations through community service, veteran support, and youth programs. At Post 6, the SAL is active in fundraising events including the annual Pancake Breakfast.

🏍️

American Legion Riders

Motorcycle enthusiasts who raise funds and awareness for veteran causes. Membership is open to American Legion members, Auxiliary members, and SAL members who own or co-own a motorcycle.

The Riders participate in parades, charity runs, and honor escort missions throughout the state, representing Post No. 6 with pride on the road.

🚗

Post 6 Cruisers

A vehicle enthusiast group connected to the Post, bringing together members who share a passion for classic and specialty automobiles.

The Cruisers support veteran outreach and community events, using their gatherings to raise visibility for the Post and its mission in the Chapel Hill area.

🎖️

Post 6 Honor Guard

An elite ceremonial unit recognized as the "Gold Standard" of Department of North Carolina Honor Guards. The Honor Guard performs at military funerals, dedications, post meetings, and community events.

All Post No. 6 members are eligible and encouraged to join. The Honor Guard ensures that every veteran's service is recognized with dignity and respect.

🎤

Speakers' Bureau

Post members who are available to speak at schools, civic organizations, and community events about the American Legion's mission, veteran issues, and the history of Post No. 6.

The Speakers' Bureau helps build connections between veterans and the broader community, educating the public on the sacrifices and service of America's veterans.

Post No. 6 opens its doors to a broad coalition of veteran, civic, and community organizations. Sharing our space is part of how we serve.

🎖️

VFW Post 9100 — C.V. Cummings

Veterans of Foreign Wars Post 9100, named for C.V. Cummings, shares the Post No. 6 facility and serves Chapel Hill's combat veteran community.

The VFW focuses specifically on veterans who served in foreign wars, complementing the American Legion's broader membership. The shared facility strengthens the bond between both organizations.

⚓

U.S. Coast Guard Auxiliary

A civilian volunteer corps supporting the U.S. Coast Guard in safety, education, and operational support missions in the region.

The Auxiliary uses Post No. 6 for meetings and coordination, reflecting the Post's commitment to supporting all branches of the armed services and their affiliated organizations.

🇺🇸

Vietnam Veterans Association

Dedicated to the needs of Vietnam-era veterans and their families — ensuring their service and sacrifice are never forgotten and that they receive the recognition they deserve.

The VVA meets at Post No. 6, which is honored to provide a home for these veterans and to support the unique challenges they continue to face.

🎣

Project Healing Waters

A fly-fishing therapy program for wounded and disabled veterans and active military personnel, partnering with the VA Medical Center in Durham.

Project Healing Waters uses Post No. 6 as a meeting and coordination point. The program provides free fly-fishing equipment, instruction, and outings to help veterans with their physical and emotional recovery.

🏕️

Boy Scout Troops 505 & Crew 505

Chartered and sponsored by Post No. 6, helping shape the next generation of leaders through outdoor adventure, civic service, and character development.

The Post's sponsorship of Troop 505 and Venturing Crew 505 reflects the American Legion's deep commitment to youth programs and ensuring that young people develop into responsible, patriotic citizens.

🎵

Bluegrass Jamboree — Fisher House

A music fundraising project benefiting the Fisher House at Fort Bragg, which provides free lodging for families of hospitalized veterans and service members.

The Bluegrass Jamboree uses Post No. 6 facilities to host concerts and events, bringing the community together through music while raising critical funds for veteran families in their time of need.

🚒

Chapel Hill Fire Department

The Chapel Hill Fire Department uses Post No. 6 facilities for training operations and exercises, strengthening the relationship between the Post and local first responders.

This partnership reflects Post No. 6's commitment to serving the broader community and supporting those who protect Chapel Hill every day.

🐕

Chapel Hill Police Department

The Chapel Hill Police Department's canine unit conducts training on Post No. 6 grounds, supporting law enforcement readiness and community safety.

Post No. 6 is proud to provide space that helps keep Chapel Hill's officers and their K-9 partners trained and prepared to protect the community.

⚡

Duke Power — Emergency Preparedness

Duke Power uses Post No. 6 facilities for emergency preparedness planning and training sessions, reflecting the Post's commitment to community resilience and readiness.

This partnership ensures that regional utility emergency response teams have a well-equipped, central location to coordinate their preparedness efforts for the Chapel Hill area.
