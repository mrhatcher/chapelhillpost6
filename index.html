Our Purpose

Standing on Four Pillars of Service

Since 1919, the American Legion has been guided by four core commitments. Post No. 6 lives these values every day in Chapel Hill and Orange County.

01
Veterans Affairs & Rehabilitation
Ensuring every veteran receives the care and benefits they earned.
02
National Security
Supporting a strong, well-prepared national defense.
03
Americanism
Promoting patriotism, youth programs, and civic responsibility.
04
Children & Youth
Investing in the next generation through scouting, Boys State, and scholarships.

Programs & Organizations

From youth development to veteran support, there's a place for every veteran and family member at Post No. 6.

🎖️

American Legion Auxiliary

Open to spouses, daughters, granddaughters, and other female relatives of veterans. The Auxiliary is one of the largest patriotic service organizations for women in the world.

Who Can Be a Member?

Congress has broadened eligibility. If you served even one day on active federal service since December 7, 1941 and received an honorable discharge — you qualify.

🎖️

Legion Member

Any honorably discharged veteran who served on active federal service since Dec. 7, 1941. All branches welcome — Army, Navy, Marines, Air Force, Coast Guard, Space Force.

$60 / year
🌟

American Legion Auxiliary

Spouses, daughters, granddaughters, sisters, mothers, and grandmothers of eligible veterans. One of the largest women's patriotic organizations in the world.

Contact Us
👦

Sons of the American Legion

Male descendants (sons, grandsons, great-grandsons, stepsons, and adopted sons) of members or deceased veterans eligible for Legion membership.

$25 / year
